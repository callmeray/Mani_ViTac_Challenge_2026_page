Overview

Building on the 2024 and 2025 editions, the 2026 challenge expands to bimanual tactile manipulation with real-robot evaluation across two contact-rich laboratory tasks.

ManiSkill-ViTac Challenge 2026 provides a standardized benchmarking platform for contact-rich tactile manipulation skill learning. Built on the ViTaMIn-B visuo-tactile bimanual platform, it unifies hardware configuration, data format and evaluation protocol to allow direct comparison of visuo-tactile policies across teams.

Participants collect real-robot demonstration data, develop visuo-tactile manipulation policies, and are evaluated on physical robots. The challenge targets core capabilities in tactile perception, contact-rich manipulation and bimanual coordination that are difficult to benchmark with vision alone.

Visuo-Tactile Policy Learning

Agents must fuse visual and tactile observations to perform contact-rich bimanual tasks that require tactile feedback for reliable execution.

Real Demonstration Data

Participants work from real-robot demonstration data collected on the ViTaMIn-B platform, covering bimanual coordination and contact-rich interactions.

Unified Real-Robot Evaluation

All teams are evaluated on a shared physical hardware setup, providing consistent and reproducible performance measurement across submissions.

Challenge Format

Inspired by more complete challenge sites, the 2026 competition is organized as a two-phase pipeline from open preparation to final real-robot evaluation.

Open Preparation and Screening

Tracks

The 2026 edition features two laboratory manipulation tracks, both evaluated on physical robots and requiring visuo-tactile policies for contact-rich task execution.

Track 1

Test Tube Pick and Place + Cleaning

Participants develop policies for picking, placing and rack-inserting glass test tubes, followed by internal cleaning with a brush. Tactile sensing is essential for compliant grasping and precise insertion.

  • Test tube pick-and-place and rack insertion
  • Internal tube cleaning with a brush
  • Evaluation on generalization across tube sizes and rack configurations
Track 2

Grinding Operation

Participants develop policies for mortar-and-pestle grinding in laboratory settings. The task requires contact force regulation and bimanual coordination, with tactile feedback as the primary control signal.

  • Force-regulated grinding with mortar and pestle
  • Bimanual coordination under contact-rich conditions
  • Evaluation on grinding quality and task completion
