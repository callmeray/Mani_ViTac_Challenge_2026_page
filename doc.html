Challenge Manual

ManiSkill-ViTac Challenge 2026

This page is the central reference for the AI for Science themed challenge structure, task scope, evaluation criteria and operational planning notes currently available to participants.

📘 Overview

ManiSkill-ViTac Challenge 2026 focuses on contact-rich bimanual manipulation with visual and tactile sensing, with an application emphasis on laboratory-style embodied manipulation. The current planning materials highlight AI for Science style tasks that require dexterous control, stable contact and reliable execution under varying materials and tools.

Compared with last year's page, the 2026 edition is being organized around a clearer participant journey: understand the benchmark, register a team, submit an early proposal package, iterate with released data and baselines, then enter the formal evaluation cycle and final workshop presentation.

This documentation reflects the latest planning notes currently available. Items marked as pending are intentionally left flexible until the final public rulebook is released.

🧭 Challenge Format

Stage A: Registration and Proposal

Teams first register through a Google Form style signup flow and prepare basic proof materials or a short proposal before the first screening deadline.

Stage B: Preparation and Iteration

Organizers release dataset examples, training baselines, tutorials and system documentation so teams can begin development before the formal submission stage.

Stage C: Formal Evaluation

Teams enter the official evaluation loop with real-time feedback and a limited number of submission opportunities, followed by final result announcement and workshop presentation.

  • Early screening: planning notes suggest a proposal review window in late April to early May 2026.
  • Offline testing target: internal notes mention a first-stage deadline before May 1, 2026 for offline testing readiness and supporting materials.
  • Evaluation quota: a real-time evaluation phase with up to 5 attempts is currently under consideration.

🧪 Tracks

The 2026 edition is organized around two concrete laboratory manipulation tracks. This section summarizes the task content of each track.

Track 1: Test Tube Pick and Place + Cleaning

Task content: complete test tube pick-and-place, insertion and cleaning tasks in laboratory settings.
  • Test tube pick and place or insertion tasks
  • Internal tube cleaning with a brush

Track 2: Grinding Operation

Task content: complete grinding tasks with mortar-and-pestle style tools in laboratory settings.
  • Grinding tasks with laboratory tools and materials
  • Mortar-and-pestle style tool use

📏 Evaluation and Scoring

The current planning document proposes a multi-factor evaluation instead of success rate alone. This better matches the laboratory setting, where speed, stability and material safety all matter.

Core Metrics

  • Task completion accuracy
  • Completion time
  • Operational stability
  • Whether drops, spills or damage occur
  • Robustness to difficult cases and disturbances

Practical Interpretation

  • Fast but unstable behavior should not dominate the ranking.
  • Material-sensitive tasks should penalize spills or destructive interactions.
  • Generalization across vessel sizes, materials and contents is expected to matter.

📝 Registration Design

The planning notes recommend a Google Form based registration channel. The public signup page has not been released yet, but the intended form structure is already clear enough to document.

Team Information

  • Team name
  • Team size, suggested range 1-5
  • Participating track selection: Track 1 for test tube pick and place plus cleaning, or Track 2 for grinding

Team Leader Information

  • Full name
  • Email address
  • Affiliation or institution
  • Country or region

Team Members

  • Repeated member fields for Member 2 to Member 5, or a simplified email list field
  • Name, email and affiliation for each additional member

Background and Compliance

  • Career stage: PhD student, postdoc, faculty, industry or other
  • Prior experience with tactile sensing or bimanual manipulation
  • How the team heard about the challenge
  • Agreement with challenge rules and code of conduct
  • Confirmation that all team members agree to participate
  • Optional hardware support or kit loan request
  • Optional brief description of the planned approach
The registration page on the website can safely expose this structure now, even if the final Google Form link is still pending.

🔧 Hardware and Materials Snapshot

The planning notes already identify example objects and consumables for the laboratory tasks. These are useful as an early signal of the intended manipulation regime, although the final public procurement list may still change.

  • Test tubes: multiple diameters, including 18 mm, 25 mm and 50 mm categories.
  • Tube racks and brushes: both handled and non-handled brushes are being considered for cleaning tasks.
  • Grinding setups: ceramic and glass mortar-and-pestle variants across different sizes.
  • Materials: calcium carbonate particles, gypsum, quartz sand, activated carbon and copper sulfate solution appear in the draft equipment list.
The website should describe categories of equipment, not internal shopping links. This page intentionally summarizes the task-relevant objects without publishing internal procurement notes.
